FAQ

1. How many blades are best for ceiling fans?

Three blades are standard because they offer good aerodynamics, but that configuration can generate wind chop in some models. Five blades are best in such instances.

2. Why is the remote control turning on and off?

Interruption or the remote tuning into another frequency may be the reason for the malfunction. Ensure no two remotes in the house are on the same frequency.

3. Why is the ceiling fan making a clicking sound?

Loose parts are the most usual causes of clicking sounds on ceiling fans. The mounting mechanisms might not be properly secured, or a screw/nut might be loose.

4. Can one remote control more than one fan?

It is possible for a remote to operate multiple ceiling fans. However, doing this comes with a risk of interfering with the frequency.

5. Can you get a custom-sized downrod?

Most brands will have downrods in standard sizes, but you can have it cut to the best size for your fan installation.

6. Why is the fan humming but not spinning when turned on?

This problem arises from a faulty capacitor. A spoilt capacitor will allow power to reach the fan but not starting the motor.

7. Do ceiling fans lose their speed over time?

Yes. The best way to tell if a fan needs replacing is to check the speed. Reduced speed may not be noticeable right away, but the air circulation will change.

8. Can you use a light fixture from a different brand?

Some ceiling fans have screws in the middle section of the motor casing that make it possible to attach a light fixture from another manufacturer. However, review the compatibility of the lighting with the fan.

10. Why does the light on the fan come on then turn off?

Some ceiling fans have wattage limits, meaning that using a bulb that exceeds it will cause a malfunction.
